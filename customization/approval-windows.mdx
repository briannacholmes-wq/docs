---
title: "Approval windows: choose when to review done tasks"
description: "Set a window of time when Our World notifies you that your child has finished a task and is waiting for your approval. Four presets plus a custom option."
---

Approval windows let you control *when* you review your child's completed quests — so you're not expected to respond in the moment. When your child marks a task as done, it waits quietly until your chosen window, then notifies you to review and approve. This removes the pressure of instant response and lets you engage on your own terms.

## Available windows

| Window | Time range | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| 🌅 Morning window | 7:00 – 9:00 AM | Starting the day with a review |
| ☀️ Midday window | 12:00 – 1:30 PM | A lunchtime check-in |
| 🌙 Evening window *(default)* | 6:00 – 8:00 PM | End-of-day approval ritual |
| ⚡ Anytime | Immediate | Notify as soon as tasks are done |
| ✏️ Custom | Your choice | Set your own start and end times |

## Setting your approval window

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Tap **Settings** (⚙️) in the bottom navigation on your parent dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your window">
    Tap the window that fits your routine. A checkmark confirms your selection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a custom time (optional)">
    To set your own window, tap the **Custom** row and enter a start and end time using the time pickers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your notification method">
    Toggle on any combination of Push, In-app, Email digest, or Silent under **Notify me via**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Tap **Save settings** to apply. Your window is saved immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  The **Evening window** works well for most families — approving tasks at dinner or bedtime turns the approval into a positive moment you share together rather than a background chore.
</Tip>

<Note>
  Tasks marked done by your child are never lost. They wait in "Waiting ⏳" status until you approve them, regardless of how much time passes.
</Note>

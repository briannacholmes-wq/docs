Every child in Our World has a virtual companion — a small digital friend that lives at the top of the child screen and grows alongside them. The companion is more than a decoration. It's a visible, ongoing reflection of the work your child puts in each day. When quests get done and parents approve them, the companion gets happier and eventually transforms into a new stage. When tasks go unfinished, the companion stays where it is, quietly waiting. It's a gentle, motivating metaphor: taking care of your responsibilities means taking care of something you love.

## The five growth stages

Every companion progresses through five stages as its happiness level rises. The stages are shown as emoji that visually change on your child's screen, so growth is something they can actually see.

| Stage | Emoji | Happiness level needed |
|-------|-------|------------------------|
| Stage 1 | 🌱 | 0 – 24 |
| Stage 2 | 🌿 | 25 – 49 |
| Stage 3 | 🌸 | 50 – 74 |
| Stage 4 | 🌺 | 75 – 99 |
| Stage 5 | 🌳 | 100 |

<Note>
  The stage emoji shown above are from the Plant companion, which uses the full plant growth sequence. Other companions follow the same five-stage progression logic at the same happiness thresholds — the visual emoji changes to match the chosen companion type.
</Note>

As the happiness level crosses each threshold, the companion's avatar on the child's screen updates automatically. Children often notice this change on their own, which is a natural opening to talk about what they accomplished.

## What makes the companion grow

The companion's happiness level increases by **12 points** each time a parent approves a quest. This happens automatically the moment you tap **Approve** on the parent side.

Here's what that looks like in practice:

- A child starts the day with a companion at 40% happiness
- They complete all 4 quests and you approve each one
- The companion gains 4 × 12 = 48 happiness points, moving from 40% to 88%
- The companion advances from Stage 2 (🌿) to Stage 4 (🌺)

The happiness level is capped at 100. If the companion is already at 100, further approvals don't increase it further — the companion simply stays at its highest stage.

## The three happiness status messages

Below the companion's name on the child's screen is a short status message that changes based on the current happiness level. These messages give children a quick read on how their companion is doing.

## Checking the companion's status

The companion card is always visible in the child's app header, just below the star bank. Your child doesn't need to navigate anywhere to check on it. The card shows:

- The companion's **avatar emoji**, which changes as it grows through stages
- The companion's **name** (chosen during onboarding)
- The **status message** reflecting its current happiness
- A **happiness bar** showing the exact percentage as a visual fill

The bar fills from left to right and uses a warm gradient from gold to green. When a parent approves a quest, the bar animates smoothly to its new value, so the growth feels immediate and satisfying.

## Tips for parents

<Tip>
  Mention the companion by name when you approve a quest. "I just approved your reading — Mochi is getting happier!" creates a direct connection between the task and the reward that children respond to much more strongly than abstract praise.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  If your child is struggling to start their tasks, ask how their companion is doing. A child who is attached to their companion often finds "let's get Mochi to the next stage today" more motivating than "you need to do your tasks." The companion gives you a shared language for talking about effort.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  The companion is a good conversation starter at the end of the day. Looking at where the companion landed — and what quests drove the growth — gives children a concrete, visual summary of how their day went.
</Tip>

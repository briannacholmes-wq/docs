Tasks and approvals are the engine that makes Our World run. You set tasks, your child works through them and marks them done, and you confirm the work with a single tap. Every approval is a small moment of recognition that translates into something tangible for your child: stars, screen time, and a companion that visibly grows. The loop is simple by design, because it has to work on a real family schedule — in the few seconds between making breakfast and heading out the door.

## How the flow works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tasks appear on your home screen">
    When you open the parent side of Our World, your child's tasks are listed on the home dashboard. Each task shows its name, how many stars it's worth, and a button reflecting its current status. Tasks don't need to be individually assigned each day — the list is ready when you are.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Your child marks a task done">
    On your child's side, tasks appear as quests. When your child finishes something, they tap the task to mark it done. Their button changes to **Waiting ⏳** and a confirmation toast appears: "Marked done! Waiting for approval."

    At the same moment, the task button on your side of the app changes to **Review!** and starts pulsing. The subheading under your name in the parent header updates to show how many tasks are waiting — for example, "3 tasks need attention."
  </Step>
  <Step title="You review and approve">
    Tap **Review!** (or **Approve** if you want to approve directly without waiting for your child) to complete the task. Stars are awarded, screen time is added, and the companion grows.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  You can approve a task at any time — even before your child marks it done on their side. This is useful if you watched the task happen in person and want to reward it immediately.
</Note>

## The three task statuses

Every task is always in one of three states. Understanding these makes it easy to scan the home dashboard and know exactly where each task stands.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Approve — task is pending" icon="circle" defaultOpen>
    The task has been assigned and is waiting. Your child has not marked it done yet. The button appears in an orange tint and reads **Approve**.

    You can tap **Approve** at any time to approve a task directly, regardless of whether your child has marked it done on their side. This is handy if you're present when the task gets done and want to skip the back-and-forth.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Review! — child marked it done, waiting for you" icon="circle-dot">
    Your child has tapped the task on their quest list and flagged it as complete. The button pulses and reads **Review!** in amber.

    This is the state that needs your attention. When you see it, your child is waiting. Tap the button to confirm the work and release the rewards.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Approved ✓ — complete" icon="check-circle">
    You've approved the task. The button reads **Approved ✓** in green and can no longer be tapped. The task row dims and the task name gains a strikethrough, making it easy to see at a glance which tasks are done for the day.

    Stars have already been awarded and screen time has already been added. There is nothing further to do for this task.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What approving a task unlocks

Each approval triggers three things at once, immediately after you tap the button.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Stars" icon="star" href="/features/home-dashboard">
    Your child's star total increases by the number of stars the task is worth. Tasks are worth 1, 2, or 3 stars depending on the effort involved. A toast confirms the amount: "⭐ Senna earned 3 stars!"
  </Card>
  <Card title="Screen time" icon="timer" href="/features/home-dashboard">
    Your child earns 5 minutes of screen time for each star. A 1-star task adds 5 minutes; a 3-star task adds 15 minutes. The total appears in the star bank on your child's header.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Companion growth" icon="heart" href="/features/companion">
    The companion's happiness level increases by 12 points. As the level climbs, the companion visually evolves — from a seedling to a full plant, for example — which your child can see immediately on their side.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Star values

Tasks in the default list carry the following star values, which reflect the relative effort of each task:

| Task | Stars |
|------|-------|
| Make bed 🛏️ | ⭐ (1 star) |
| Morning routine ☀️ | ⭐⭐ (2 stars) |
| Clean up toys 🧸 | ⭐⭐ (2 stars) |
| Reading 20 min 📖 | ⭐⭐⭐ (3 stars) |

The star value is always visible next to the task name on both the parent and child sides of the app, so your child always knows what they're working toward.

## Tips for using approval windows

You don't need to approve tasks the moment they come in. Our World lets you set a specific time window when you review completed tasks — so your child knows not to expect an instant response during school hours or your work block.

<Tip>
  Setting an approval window for the evening means your child experiences the satisfaction of completing tasks during the day and receives the reward together at a predictable time — which tends to feel more meaningful than an instant, distracted tap.
</Tip>

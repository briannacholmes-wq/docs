The quests tab is your child's main view in Our World. It turns the day's tasks into a straightforward, rewarding loop: tap a quest to say you've done it, wait for a parent to confirm, and watch your star count rise. For parents, understanding how the quests screen looks from your child's perspective makes it easier to talk about tasks together and set expectations that feel fair to everyone.

## The family agreement

At the top of the quests screen is a banner called **Our deal**. It shows the agreement your family made together — for example:

> **Our deal:** Finish all 4 quests and we pick a fun Friday activity together. You signed it! 📝

This banner stays visible every time your child opens the quests tab. It's a gentle, non-nagging reminder of the bigger picture: the tasks aren't just chores, they're the path to something you chose together. Kids who helped decide what the reward is tend to stay more motivated throughout the week.

<Note>
  The family agreement text is set up during onboarding and reflects the deal your family agreed to. It's displayed as a read-only banner — neither parent nor child can change it from within the quests tab.
</Note>

## Today's quests

Below the agreement banner is the **Today's quests ⚡** section. Each quest is a card showing:

- A colored icon indicating the type of task (☀️ for morning routine, 📖 for reading, 🛏️ for make bed, 🧸 for clean up toys)
- The task name
- The star value and approval note — for example, **+2 ⭐ · Mom approves**
- A status badge showing where the task stands right now

## How to complete a quest

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the quest you finished">
    Look at your quest list. Any quest showing an orange **Tap done!** badge is ready to mark complete. That means you've done the task and haven't told the app yet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the quest card">
    Tap anywhere on the quest card. The app records that you're done and shows a confirmation message at the bottom of the screen: "Marked done! Waiting for approval."

    The badge on the quest changes from **Tap done!** to **Waiting ⏳** in yellow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for your parent to approve">
    Your parent will see the task flagged on their side of the app. When they approve it, the badge turns green and reads **Approved ✓**. Your stars and screen time are added immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If a quest already shows **Waiting ⏳** or **Approved ✓**, tapping it won't do anything — the app knows you've already marked it done. You can't accidentally un-complete a quest.
</Tip>

## The three quest statuses

Every quest is always in one of three states. Here's what each badge means:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Tap done! — you haven't marked it yet" icon="circle" defaultOpen>
    The quest is waiting for you. You haven't tapped it yet, which means either you haven't done the task or you forgot to mark it.

    The badge is orange. When you're ready, tap the card to tell the app you're done.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Waiting ⏳ — you marked it, parent hasn't approved yet" icon="circle-dot">
    You tapped the quest and told the app you finished. Now you're waiting for your parent to check and approve it from their side.

    The badge is yellow. This is totally normal — your parent might be at work or busy, and they'll get to it when they can.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Approved ✓ — done and rewarded" icon="check-circle">
    Your parent approved the quest. Your stars and screen time have already been added, and the companion gained happiness too.

    The badge is green. The task name gets a strikethrough to show it's fully complete. Well done!
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Stars and what they earn you

Every approved quest adds stars to your total for the day. Stars do two things:

- They build up in your **star bank**, shown at the top of the child screen
- They unlock **screen time** — you earn 5 minutes for every star

So if a quest is worth 3 stars and your parent approves it, you instantly earn 3 stars and 15 minutes of screen time. The numbers update right away so you can see the change.

## Your star bank

The star bank is the orange card at the top of your screen. It always shows:

- Your **total stars earned today** as a big number on the left
- A short motivational message below it — for example, "Keep going — almost at 10!"
- Your **screen time minutes** in a pill on the right

## The default quests

These are the four quests that appear in the app by default. Your family may use all of them or just some, depending on your routine.

| Quest | Icon | Stars |
|-------|------|-------|
| Morning routine | ☀️ | 2 stars |
| Reading 20 min | 📖 | 3 stars |
| Make bed | 🛏️ | 1 star |
| Clean up toys | 🧸 | 2 stars |

<Note>
  Stars reflect how much effort a task takes. Reading for 20 minutes is worth 3 stars because it takes focus and time. Making bed is worth 1 star because it's quick — but it still counts, and every star adds up.
</Note>

## Tips for parents

<Tip>
  Talk through the star bank with your child at the start of the day. Knowing that 8 stars = 40 minutes of screen time gives them a concrete goal to work toward, which is more motivating than an abstract "do your tasks."
</Tip>

<Tip>
  If you approve a task before your child marks it done — for example, because you watched them do it in person — the quest will jump straight to **Approved ✓** on their side. This is fine and intentional. See the [tasks and approvals guide](/features/tasks-approvals) for more on when to do this.
</Tip>

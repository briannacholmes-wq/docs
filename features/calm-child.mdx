The calm tab is your child's quiet corner in Our World. When something feels big — whether you're frustrated, restless, anxious, or just need a moment — the calm tab has short, friendly activities to help feelings settle. Each one takes just a few minutes, and you can do them anywhere: on the couch, in your room, or even outside. You don't have to feel bad to use them; they work even better when you use them regularly.

## The six calm activities

Scroll through the activity cards at the top of the calm tab to see all six options. Each card shows an icon, the activity name, and how long it takes. Tap any card to start.

| Icon | Activity | Duration | What it does |
|------|----------|----------|--------------|
| 🫧 | Bubble Breath | 2 min | Slow, calming breaths that help you settle when you're feeling wound up |
| 🕺 | Shake It Out | 1 min | Move your body to shake off big feelings — great when you're angry or antsy |
| ⭐ | Star Breathing | 3 min | A structured breathing exercise that brings you back to a calm, focused place |
| 🧸 | Cozy Corner | 5 min | A longer, gentler rest with soothing guidance — good for winding down or feeling overwhelmed |
| 🌈 | Rainbow Walk | 4 min | A mindful activity that helps you pay attention to what's around you |
| 🎵 | Hum & Hug | 2 min | Gentle humming and self-hugging to feel safe and comforted |

<Note>
  The activities are shown in a horizontally scrolling row. If you can only see a few cards at first, swipe left to see the rest.
</Note>

## How to start an activity

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the calm tab">
    Tap the **Calm** tab (🫧) in the bottom navigation bar on your child's screen. The activity cards appear at the top under the heading **Feel better right now 🌟**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose an activity">
    Tap any activity card. The card highlights when you touch it. You don't need to decide in advance which one is right — you can try one and come back to try another.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Follow the breathing overlay">
    A full-screen session opens with the activity name, a breathing guide, and a countdown. Follow along at your own pace. Tap **End session** when you're ready to stop.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The breathing overlay

When you start any activity, the screen dims and a breathing guide fills the display. This is called the meditation overlay. It works the same way for every activity.

Here's what you'll see:

- The **activity name** at the top
- A **breathing ring** in the center of the screen — a circle that expands when you should breathe in, and contracts when you should breathe out
- The **current phase** shown inside the ring
- A **countdown number** that ticks down each second
- An **"End session"** button at the bottom if you want to stop early

### The four breathing phases

The overlay guides you through a repeating cycle of four phases, one after the other:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Breathe in — 4 seconds" icon="arrow-up" defaultOpen>
    The ring expands. Inhale slowly through your nose. The counter starts at 4 and counts down to 1. The instruction reads: "Inhale slowly through your nose."
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Hold — 7 seconds" icon="pause">
    The ring stays expanded. Keep the air in gently — don't force it. The counter counts down from 7. The instruction reads: "Hold still, be gentle."
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Breathe out — 8 seconds" icon="arrow-down">
    The ring contracts. Let the air out slowly. This is the longest phase and the one that does the most to calm your nervous system. The instruction reads: "Let it all go slowly."
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Rest — 4 seconds" icon="minus">
    The ring is small. Just be here. The instruction reads: "Rest and be here." After this phase, the cycle starts again from **Breathe in**.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  It's okay if the hold or the out-breath feel long at first. You don't have to match the timer exactly — just use it as a guide and breathe at a pace that feels comfortable.
</Tip>

## Did you know?

The calm tab includes a science fact card below the activity scroll. It explains in child-friendly language why these activities actually work:

- When you take 3 deep breaths, your brain calms down — like pressing a reset button
- Moving your body for just 1 minute when you're upset can help your feelings feel smaller

These facts are there to help children understand that what they're doing isn't just a trick — it's something their brain and body respond to in a real, measurable way.

## Tips for parents

<Tip>
  The calm activities work best when children already know them before they need them. Encourage your child to try one or two activities on a neutral day — not just when they're upset. Once the activities feel familiar, your child is much more likely to reach for them in a difficult moment.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  If your child is overwhelmed or refusing to engage, you can do a calm activity together. Opening the overlay side by side, even without the screen, can normalize the practice and make it feel less like a correction and more like something the family does.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  The **Shake It Out** activity (1 minute) is especially useful for children who are physically agitated — running in place, unable to sit still, or dealing with big anger. Physical movement before a breathing exercise often makes the breathing land better.
</Tip>

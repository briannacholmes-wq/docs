The Memory Keeper is a private journal for the two of you. It lives in the **💛 Memories** tab on both the parent and child sides of the app, and its purpose is simple: to hold the moments that would otherwise slip away. A funny thing your child said at dinner. The day they finished their first chapter book. The small, ordinary afternoon that somehow felt like everything. You don't need to write much — a title and a sentence or two is enough to make a moment retrievable years from now.

## How to add a moment

Tap the **+ Add moment** button in the top-right corner of the Memories screen to open the add form. Fill in as much or as little as you like, then tap **Save ✦**.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Give the moment a title">
    The title field is required. Make it specific enough that you'll know what you're reading about — "Said 'I love you' first tonight" is more useful later than "A good moment." A short, vivid title is all you need.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tell the story">
    The story field is an open text area where you can write as much or as little as you want. Describe what happened, what your child said, how it felt. Two or three sentences is plenty. The story is displayed in the memory feed with a two-line preview — but the full text is always there when you tap in.

    <Tip>
      The more specific your story, the more you'll value it later. Include a detail: what room you were in, what she was wearing, the exact words they used. These are the things you'll forget first.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a date">
    A date picker defaults to today's date. Change it if you're recording something that happened earlier — catching up on a moment from last week is just as worthwhile as logging it in real time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose one or more categories">
    Select the categories that best describe the moment. You can choose multiple. Categories make it easy to filter memories later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Tap **Save ✦**. The moment appears at the top of the memory feed immediately, with its icon, title, date, a two-line preview of your story, and its category tags.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The 8 memory categories

Each category is designed to capture a different dimension of childhood and family life. You can assign more than one to any memory.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="🌱 Growth" icon="seedling">
    Moments where your child stretched beyond their comfort zone, tried something hard, or showed you who they're becoming. Standing up for a friend, handling disappointment with grace, trying something they were afraid of.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="💛 Emotional" icon="heart">
    The tender, vulnerable moments — and the ones that caught you off guard. Unprompted "I love yous," big feelings processed well, conversations that went deeper than you expected.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="🏆 Achievement" icon="trophy">
    Milestones and accomplishments worth marking: finishing a chapter book, a test they studied hard for, a goal they set and met. Academic wins, personal bests, and firsts.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="🤣 Funny" icon="face-grin-tears">
    The things that made you laugh out loud. The weird logic of a seven-year-old, the accidental observation, the joke that didn't land but the delivery was perfect. Save these — they're the ones you'll read out loud someday.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="💬 First time" icon="message">
    Anything that happened for the first time. First time riding a bike, first time cooking something, first time staying home alone, first time admitting they were wrong. Firsts are finite — each one only happens once.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="🤝 Us" icon="handshake">
    Moments that belong to the two of you together. Something you built, a conversation only you two had, a tradition you stumbled into. The memories that are specifically about your relationship with each other.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="📚 School" icon="book">
    Anything school-related worth preserving: a project they were proud of, a teacher who said something meaningful, a subject that suddenly clicked, a friendship formed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="💫 Magical" icon="stars">
    The moments that don't fit anywhere else — the ones that felt inexplicably special. A coincidence, a quiet afternoon that turned golden, something you couldn't have planned if you tried.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Browsing and filtering memories

The memory feed shows all saved moments in reverse chronological order by default, with the most recent at the top. Each card shows:

- A **category icon** in a soft color block on the left
- The **title** and **date** on the same line
- A **two-line preview** of your story
- **Category tags** along the bottom of the card

To narrow the feed, use the filter chips above it:

| Filter | Shows |
|--------|-------|
| All | Every memory |
| 🌱 Growth | Growth moments only |
| 💛 Emotional | Emotional moments only |
| 🏆 Achievement | Achievement moments only |
| 🤣 Funny | Funny moments only |
| 🤝 Us | Moments tagged as "Us" |

A count at the bottom of the screen shows the total number of moments saved — a small but meaningful number to watch grow.

## Your child's perspective

Your child can also view shared memories from the **💛 Memories** tab on their side of the app. They see the same feed of moments you've saved — a reminder that these experiences matter and are worth keeping.

Your child can also tap **+ Add a moment** from their side of the app. When they do, the memory is submitted for your review and appears in your Memory Keeper after you approve it.

<Note>
  Memories your child adds are sent to you for approval before they appear in the shared feed. You'll see a notification so you don't miss them.
</Note>
